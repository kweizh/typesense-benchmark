### How the three-signal ranking is encoded

```
sort_by = _eval([(badge:=sponsored):3,(badge:=featured):2,(badge:=none):1]):desc
        , _text_match(buckets:10):desc
        , popularity:desc
```

**Signal 1 — Promotion tier (`_eval`)**  
`_eval()` assigns a conditional integer score per document at query time without a stored numeric field. `sponsored→3`, `featured→2`, `none→1`, sorted descending, so sponsored always floats above featured, which floats above untagged — regardless of text relevance.

**Signal 2 — Text relevance (`_text_match` + `max_score` mode)**  
The search parameter `text_match_type=max_score` makes Typesense accumulate match quality across *all* queried fields (`title,description`), so a document matching in both fields scores higher than one matching in only one. The `buckets:10` argument bucketises raw scores into 10 discrete levels, giving clean discrimination within a tier while keeping the sort slot count at 3 (Typesense's maximum).

**Signal 3 — Popularity tiebreaker**  
`popularity:desc` resolves exact text-score ties within a tier by favouring the more popular item.
